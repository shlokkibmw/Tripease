Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time of year to visit the monasteries in Sikkim?
The ideal times are March to May (spring) and October to mid-December (autumn). During these months, the weather is generally clear and stable, offering spectacular mountain views and pleasant conditions for travel. The monsoon season (June-September) can bring landslides and obscured views, while winter (Jan-Feb) offers a unique, serene beauty but some high-altitude monasteries may be difficult to access due to snow.
Can I attend prayer ceremonies as a tourist?
Yes, many monasteries welcome respectful visitors to observe their prayer ceremonies. The best times to witness these are usually early in the morning (around 7 AM) and late afternoon (around 3-4 PM). Major festivals like Losar (Tibetan New Year) and Pang Lhabsol are spectacular times to visit, featuring vibrant Cham (masked) dances. It's always best to check the specific timings with your hotel or a local guide.
Are there any dress codes or etiquette I should follow when visiting a monastery?
Yes, respecting local customs is crucial. Please follow these guidelines: 1.Dress Modestly: Cover your shoulders and knees. Avoid wearing shorts, sleeveless tops, or revealing clothing. 2.Remove Your Shoes: Always take off your shoes before entering the main prayer hall (Dukhang). 3.Be Respectfully Quiet: Speak in a low voice and avoid disruptive behavior. 4.Ask Before Photographing: It is often prohibited to take photos or videos inside the main prayer halls, especially of the deities and during prayers. Always look for signs or ask a monk for permission. 5.Circumambulate Clockwise: When walking around stupas or prayer wheels, always move in a clockwise direction.
Are there places to stay near the major monasteries?
Accommodation options vary, with a full range of hotels available in gateway towns like Gangtok for Rumtek Monastery and Pelling for Pemayangtse Monastery, while more remote locations like Tashiding sometimes offer very basic monastery guesthouses or homestays for pilgrims seeking an immersive spiritual experience, providing a unique opportunity to live within the tranquil monastic community.
Do I need any special permits to visit the monasteries?
While monasteries in and around Gangtok like Rumtek and Enchey do not require special permits, visiting those in protected border areas of North and East Sikkim does; Indian nationals need an Inner Line Permit (ILP), and foreign nationals must obtain a Protected Area Permit (PAP), both of which can be easily arranged through registered travel agents in Gangtok, making it crucial to plan your itinerary in advance to ensure access to all the sites you wish to see.
